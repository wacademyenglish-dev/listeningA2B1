The Big Debate

What is your biggest enemy when listening to English? Qual é o seu maior inimigo ao ouvir inglês?

Speed! They speak 200km/h! Velocidade! Falam a 200km/h!

Weird Accents! Sotaques esquisitos!

Unknown Words! Palavras desconhecidas!

Teacher: Great point! Let's see if you can survive the blind test regardless of these enemies.

Phase 1: The Blind Test

No subtitles. No hints. Just you and the audio. Sem legendas. Sem dicas. Apenas você e o áudio.

"You will hear 5 fast phrases from a native speaker. Try to understand what he is saying. Don't worry if it's hard!"

Blind Test 1/5

Listen carefully. What did he say? Ouça com atenção. O que ele disse?

What did you hear?

Select the correct phrase: Selecione a frase correta:

Yes! He connects the words very fast ("I dunno whatcha think").

Connected Speech

This is what he actually said! Isto é o que ele realmente disse!

Textbook English:

I don't know what you think.

Fast Native English:

"I dunno whatcha think."

Blind Test 2/5

Listen carefully. Ouça com atenção.

Dictation Station

Build the exact sentence he said! Construa a frase exata que ele disse!

Perfect! He swallowed "going to" into "gonna". Let's continue.

Connected Speech

Notice how "going to" and "want to" disappear! Note como "going to" e "want to" desaparecem!

Textbook English:

I was going to grab a bite. Want to come?

Fast Native English:

"I was gonna grab a bite. Wanna come?"

Blind Test 3/5

Listen carefully. Ouça com atenção.

Bug Hunt

Tap the wrong word based on what he said. Toque na palavra errada com base no que ele disse.

I was kind of hoping you say that.

Yes! He said "You'd" (You would). That small 'd' makes a huge difference.

Connected Speech

"Kind of" and the hidden 'd'. "Kind of" e o 'd' escondido.

Textbook English:

I was kind of hoping you would say that.

Fast Native English:

"I was kinda hoping you'd say that."

Blind Test 4/5

Listen carefully to the idiom. Ouça com atenção a expressão idiomática.

What did he mean?

He said: "He ended up backing out at the last minute."
What does "back out" mean? O que significa "back out"?

Correct! It means to cancel.

Blind Test 5/5

The final and hardest one. A última e mais difícil.

What did he mean?

He said: "...but it might not pan out the way you expect."
What does "pan out" mean? O que significa "pan out"?

Brilliant! "Pan out" means to develop or turn out well. Let's decode everything now.

Real Life Reaction

Your friend backs out of a dinner plan 10 minutes before. What do you do? Seu amigo desmarca um jantar faltando 10 minutos. O que você faz?

Get very angry and complain! Fica bravo e reclama!

Say "No problem" but feel sad. Diz "Sem problema" mas fica triste.

Feel happy! I can stay home! Fica feliz! Posso ficar em casa!

Teacher: Discuss their choice! Have they ever backed out of a plan just to stay home?

The Native Texting

Invite your friend to eat. Send the message that sounds like a NATIVE! Convide seu amigo para comer. Envie a mensagem que soa como um NATIVO!

Friend
Hey! I'm so hungry right now.
NATIVE VERIFIED

Awesome! Way more natural. Ask the student: Where do you like to grab a bite?

Context Clues

Read the real-world situation and select the best phrase. Leia a situação real e escolha a frase exata.

"You planned a big party, but things went completely wrong and the results were bad. What happened?"

Brilliant! Have things ever "not panned out" for you on a trip?

Honesty Hour

A native speaker is talking too fast. You missed 50% of what they said. What do you do? Um nativo fala rápido demais. Você perdeu 50% do que ele disse. O que você faz?

Nod, smile, and pretend I understood. Aceno, sorrio e finjo que entendi.

Stop them and say: "Can you repeat?" Paro e peço: "Pode repetir?"

Panic and run away! Entro em pânico e fujo!

Teacher: It is totally normal! Teach them polite ways to interrupt: "Sorry, I didn't catch that."
